The sun rises from the east, accompanied by the singing birds and the vibrant hues of the sky. In the middle of the lush forest lies a small town, name remaining forgotten.

In the middle of the town stood a giant narra tree — its wooden bark, rough and old, doesn’t mellow even when strong winds bluster or the rain turns to storm. Thick green vines hang from each of its branches, and moss grows with life. 

A giant tree older than the town itself.

“Belle! Don’t let me catch ye staring at that tree again.” The peace was broken by the scream of an old hag.

“Sometimes I wonder if that hag is older than the tree itself—ack!” Belle grumbled with furrowed brows before being hit by a wooden spoon.

“I heard that ye punk. No respect for elders tis days.” The old woman crossed her arms, eyes blazing. “If you don’t start doing yer chores I’ll burn that tree down.”

“Mama! That’s taboo.”

“Blah blah blah. As if I ever cared about that old coot’s rule.”

Belle gave a blank stare.

‘You’re an old coot too!’ 

“Anyway, Belle — don’t forget to take out the weeds from that old tree. The old coot will drive us out if we miss a chore.”

“You do know that the ‘old coot’ you’re talking about is your husband.”

Belle took a wooden bucket full of tasty snacks, preparing herself for the arduous chore. Weed-pulling.

She looked left and right before pulling out a small leather book from the shelf and putting it quickly in her dress pocket.

“Should have divorced ‘im! Never marry a stuck-up, Belle — or you’ll look like me. Ka, ka, ka, ka, ka!” 

The old woman, Kaka, pointed at herself before cackling and presenting her only remaining tooth to the world.

Belle grimaced at the sight. Forcing a grin upon her face, brown eyes looking at the door.

“Okay?”

“Ye should look for a big burly hairy man—” 

Belle immediately shot to the door, goosebumps raising.

“Ahahahaha, I really got to go now!”

The wooden door creaks.

“That has a primal style to ‘em and —”

“Bye, bye! Love you!”

“Okay, have fun!” Kaka giggled, one finger touching her lips while acting cutesy. What a sight.

Outside the door Kaka’s voice is still apparent. Belle rolled her eyes.

“That old hag never changes.”

As Belle walks, she is greeted by a familiar sight. The gritty stone path reflects the vibrant blue sky. Each murky water and rusty pipes reminds Belle of home. A small town she grew up in, her small little world.

She looked left and right before taking out the small leather book from her bucket. Biting her lips in anticipation.
Its cover is worn and peeling but its title, oh its title “Beauty and the Beast.”

Belle let out a squeal akin to someone experiencing a crush, and from every line of the book she skips throughout the town.

“Hey, Belle!”

“Hi, Belle!”

“Greetings!”

Each greet leaving unanswered for Belle’s mind has long left the place. She almost didn’t stop skipping if not for the marvelous sight of the old narra tree.

“I wonder if such a beast lives up there.” 

She pursed her lips before looking around, hoping no one heard her. She sat upon the tree’s roots in contemplation. Kaka’s words about burly men echoing within her head. She sighed dreamily but quickly stopped it.

“I’m such a freak.”

She let out a raspberry while looking up to its verdant leaves, kissed by sunlight.

“Time to get to work. I hate work. Who even discovered jobs?!”

She unrolled her sleeves, fire blazing in her eyes.

“No dirt nor weed can defeat the mighty Belle! Oh uhm…sorry!”

There was a bystander. A very worried bystander.

“Are you…okay?”

“Yep. Yep. Very…cool-io?”

“I see…Nice to see you too, I guess?”
“Yeah! Ha. Ha. Bye!”

The bystander left with an awkward demeanor.

“Belle, why did you do that you idiot! Eurghh, I’m talking to myself again. Let’s do work. Work!”

Like she said, Belle worked a long time. The vibrant blue sky turns dim and accompanied now by the twinkling stars and somber clouds.

“I guess…this is fine?”

Belle weeded out the flowers too.

“Yep, all good. All done.”

However, there is another reason why Belle loves staring at this narra tree. A reason for loving beauty and the beast.

“Hey! Hey! I know you’re here!”

Ever since she was a child, she could see things that no one could see. Phantoms, dwarfs, and other creatures that never appear to normal humans.

A fascination with other realms grew budding within her heart. If she’s abnormal, isn’t it better to love someone abnormal too?

Belle took a small rock and threw it onto the tree top.

“I know you’re there! Stop ignoring me. I’ve brought food!”

To love a beast fitting for beauty.

“Belle.” A gruff voice was heard.

“You’re here.”

Upon the tree top sat a giant creature. It’s dark long hair weaving with the tree leaves and branches that was stuck for a very long time. His skin ashy and rough yet reflects the moonlight. A creature of the night. A protector of the narra tree. The Kapre.

“I told you to stop trying to court me.” Every time he spoke, large smoke puffed out of his mouth.

“Yeah, well…I’m already here.”

Kapre lowered himself in front of Belle. His size rivals that of an elephant.

“Why me?”

“Why not you? People think I’m a weirdo here, but you’re just…you.”

“So, you’re courting me for validation?”

“No! No, no, no…It’s just…” Belle finds her lips dry, speechless with the blunt question of the Kapre. She let out a big exhale.

“A lot of people here…don’t find me you know — normal. I can see things they can’t see. So I…thought maybe you’ll understand?”

“No, not really.”

“You’re really blunt.” 

“Yet you keep on courting me.” Kapre puffs out a smoke from his large tobacco. “Belle, you have people waiting for you in this town.”

“Doesn’t feel that way.”

“I’m not a marrying type.”

“We don’t need to marry.”

“I’m not a roommate type.”

“Long distance relationships exist.”

“Belle, I’m rejecting you. You know that.”

“...I don’t really have a chance?”

“Belle, you’ve lived your whole life in this small town. Get a higher standard will you?” He stared at Belle’s watery eyes, before sighing.

“The world is big, but you’re content with living in your small little world. A billion people are waiting to introduce themselves to you.”

“I have high standards! Yeah, I have really big standards that you won’t be able to keep up. You, you—”

“You’re in denial.”

“I’m heartbroken!”

“You’re got a witty heart on you Belle, people love witty.”

“I…I—”

“You keep thinking that you never fit in but when people give space for you, you ignore it. Why?”

“Didn’t know you have a therapy license.”

“Hold my hand, Belle.”

“Why?”

“Just hold it will you, girl?”

Belle took the rough hands with a feather touch. She can’t help but compare their hands.

With a large swoop, the Kapre took her to the tree top. Each branch rustling and weaving upon their body. His hands never hurt her. A feather touch for a burly creature.

“What is this…”

Every light from each house can be compared to the twinkling stars upon the sky. A cold breeze seems to hold her hands, hugging her. Each leaf of the narra tree seems to tease her just to make her laugh.

“It’s beautiful.”

“It is.”

“Why?”

“This is your little world that learned to love you, from when you were born until you’re here with me. Belle, remember this view…remember that this town learned to love you like you love me.”

“I’m scared.”

“Give them a chance, if they don’t want to give you one…remember that the world is big enough for you and I.”
